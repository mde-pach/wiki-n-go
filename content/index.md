# Welcome to Wikigit

**Wikigit** is a wiki — a website a group of people build together, one page at a time. Think of a community handbook, a game guide, a team knowledge base, or an encyclopedia like [[w:Wikipedia|Wikipedia]]. Anyone you allow can add and improve pages, right in their browser.

What sets it apart is how little there is to run and worry about. You can set one up in a few minutes without writing any code.

## What you get

test
- **Free to host.** Your wiki runs on free services — no server to rent, no monthly bill.
- **No database to manage.** Your pages are ordinary text files. Nothing can quietly break.
- **Edit in the browser.** People click *Edit*, make a change, and save — nothing to install.
- **Nothing is ever lost.** Every change is kept, and any page rolls back to an earlier version in a click.
- **It's yours.** Your content lives in your own [[w:GitHub|GitHub]] account. Export it or move it any time — nothing is locked in.
- **Looks the part.** A clean, familiar reading experience out of the box, with light and dark modes.

## Who it's for

You don't need to be a programmer. If you can copy a project on GitHub and click a couple of buttons, you can run a Wikigit.[^friction] It works well for a community or fan wiki, documentation for a project, a team knowledge base, or a personal notebook you want to publish.
