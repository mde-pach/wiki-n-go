# Administration

The **admin area** is the single place where you and your fellow maintainers run the day-to-day of a wiki. It's visible only to maintainers, and it gathers the [[trust-and-moderation|moderation]] tools into one dashboard so you're not hunting for them across the site. You'll find it at your wiki's `/admin` address, or from the page tools when you're signed in as a maintainer.

## What's in it

- **Recent activity** — a live feed of edits across the wiki, with the risky-looking ones flagged.
- **Review queue** — changes waiting for approval; see what each one does and accept it or set it aside.
- **New pages** — pages just created, so a newcomer's first article gets a quick look.
- **Protection** — set which pages are locked, and to which level.
- **Blocks** — block or unblock a disruptive contributor, across the wiki or on specific pages.
- **Editors** — see who has trusted-editor standing, and grant or remove it.
- **Auto-moderator** — review anything the automatic vandalism check has undone, and tune it.
- **Hidden items** — manage names or versions you've hidden from public lists.
- **Activity log** — a complete, searchable record of every moderation action and who took it.

## A typical routine

Most maintainers settle into a simple rhythm:

1. Skim **Recent activity** for anything out of the ordinary.
2. Clear the **review queue** — accept the good, set aside the rest.
3. Welcome and check the **new pages**.

On a quiet wiki that's a couple of minutes now and then. The flags and the auto-moderator absorb the obvious cases, so your attention goes to the edits that genuinely need a person. And because every action is logged, a team of maintainers always has a clear account of who did what.
