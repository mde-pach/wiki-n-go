# Customize

Once your wiki is running, a few touches make it feel like your own. Nearly all of them live in one place — the **Settings** screen inside your wiki — where you change values in a form. There's no code to write and no rebuild to wait for; a saved setting takes effect for the next reader. The full list of fields is in [[reference/site-settings|Site settings]]; here's what you can actually do with them.

## Name and tagline

Set the wiki's **name** so it appears in the header and the browser tab, and a short **tagline** to sit beneath it. A clear name and a one-line description do a surprising amount to make a wiki feel established rather than improvised.

## The look

Choose the defaults every visitor starts with:

- **Style** — Wikigit's own clean look, or a *Wiki* style modelled on [[w:Wikipedia|Wikipedia]].
- **Light or dark** — or automatic, following each visitor's device.
- **Width and text size** — comfortable defaults for your content.

These are starting points, not handcuffs. A reader can always override them for themselves; your settings just decide where everyone begins. (Per-page overrides are possible too — see [[reference/page-settings|Page settings]].)

## The home page

Your front page is the `index` page. Edit it like any other to welcome people and point them at your best content. There's more on this in [[help/organizing|Organizing]].

## A custom web address

A new wiki lives at `your-name.wikigit.org`. To use a domain of your own — `wiki.example.com`, say — point it at your wiki with your domain provider and connect it in settings. Most providers make this a couple of clicks.

## Languages

If your wiki will cover more than one language, turn on the ones you want here. Each page can then have versions in those languages, linked with a switcher for readers. The how and why is in [[multilingual|Multilingual wikis]].
