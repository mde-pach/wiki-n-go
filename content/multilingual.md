# Multilingual wikis

A wiki doesn't have to be in one language. Wikigit can hold the same article in several, as separate pages that know about each other, so a reader lands on the version in their language and can switch with a click. This very page, for instance, has a French counterpart, and the language bar moves between them.

## How the languages sit

One language is the wiki's default, and its pages keep plain addresses — `/coffee`. Every other language is marked by a short prefix on the address, so the French version of that page lives at `/fr/coffee` and the German at `/de/coffee`. The arrangement reads naturally in a URL and makes a page's language obvious at a glance.

The translations of one article are tied together by a shared **translation group** — a small label every version carries — so the wiki knows that `/coffee` and `/fr/coffee` are the same subject in two languages, and can offer the reader the switch.

## For readers and writers

A reader gets a **language bar** on any page that has translations, listing the languages it's available in. Where a language is *missing*, the bar doesn't go dead — it offers to start that translation, opening a new page in the right place, ready to write. A wiki fills out its languages the same organic way it fills out everything else: one page at a time, by whoever shows up able to write it.

Turning languages on for a wiki, and choosing which ones, is a site setting covered in [[help/customize|Customize]].
