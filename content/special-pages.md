# Special pages

**Special pages** are the wiki's reports about itself. They aren't articles anyone writes; they're generated from the [[links-and-references|link graph]], the [[categories|categories]], and the [[revisions|history]], and they recompute as the wiki changes. Their job is to surface the loose ends a growing wiki accumulates, so tidying up becomes a matter of working down a list rather than remembering what needs doing.

## Watching what changes

The most-used report is **recent changes** — a running feed of every edit across the wiki, newest first, with the riskier-looking ones flagged. It's where a maintainer starts the day, and where the [[trust-and-moderation|moderation]] tools hang off each entry. A related view, **new pages**, shows just the pages that have appeared, which is the natural place to give a newcomer's first article a quick look.

## Finding the loose ends

Several reports come straight out of how pages link to each other, and each points at a different kind of gap:

- **Wanted pages** — pages that are linked to but don't exist yet, ranked by how often they're asked for. This is your to-do list, written by the wiki's own [[links-and-references|red links]].
- **Orphaned pages** — pages nothing links to, which readers will struggle to ever find.
- **Dead-end pages** — pages that link nowhere themselves, and so trap a reader who lands on them.

Work those three down and the wiki stays navigable: every page reachable, every page a way onward, and the most-wanted gaps filled first.

## Browsing the whole

Other reports just help you see the shape of things — all pages, all categories, a contributor's history, the [[trust-and-moderation|review queue]]. None of it needs maintaining, because none of it is stored; it's all read back out of the content whenever you ask.
