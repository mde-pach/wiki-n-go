# Make it yours

Once your wiki is running, a few touches make it feel like your own. Most of these live in a short **settings file** in your GitHub copy — you change values, not code. The exact fields are listed in [Settings](settings.md); here's what you can do.

## Name and logo

Set your wiki's name so it appears in the header and browser tab. Replace the logo by swapping in your own image. A clear name and a simple logo go a long way toward making a wiki feel established.

## The look

Pick the defaults every visitor starts with:

- **Style** — Wikigit's own clean look, or a *Wiki* style modeled on Wikipedia.
- **Light or dark** — or automatic, following each visitor's device.
- **Text size and column width** — comfortable defaults for your content.
- **Colors** — adjust the accent and background tones to match your brand.

Visitors can always change these for themselves; your settings are just the starting point.

## The home page

Your front page is the `index` page — edit it like any other to welcome people and point them to your best content. See [Organizing your wiki](organizing.md).

## A custom web address

Your wiki starts on a free address (like `your-name.github.io/your-wiki`). To use your own domain — `wiki.yoursite.com` or `yoursite.com` — point it at your wiki with your domain provider. Most providers make this a couple of clicks; the host you publish to has a short guide for connecting a custom domain.

## Languages

If your wiki will cover more than one language, turn on the languages you want. Each page can then have versions in those languages, linked together with a language switcher for readers. See [Organizing your wiki](organizing.md#multiple-languages).
