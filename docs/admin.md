# The admin area

The **admin area** is the single place where you and your fellow maintainers run the day-to-day of the wiki. It's only visible to maintainers, and it gathers every tool from [Managing changes](managing.md) into one dashboard.

You'll find it at the `/admin` address of your wiki, or linked from the page tools when you're signed in as a maintainer.

## What's in it

- **Recent activity** — a live feed of edits across the whole wiki, with the risky-looking ones flagged.
- **Review queue** — changes waiting for approval. See what each one does and approve it or set it aside.
- **New pages** — pages just created, so you can give newcomers' work a quick look.
- **Protection** — set which pages are locked and to what level.
- **Blocks** — block or unblock a disruptive visitor, across the wiki or on specific pages.
- **Editors** — see who has trusted-editor status and grant or remove it.
- **Auto-moderator** — review anything the automatic vandalism check has undone, and tune it.
- **Hidden items** — manage names or versions you've hidden from public lists.
- **Activity log** — a complete, searchable record of every moderation action and who took it.

## A typical routine

Most maintainers settle into a simple rhythm:

1. Skim **Recent activity** for anything unusual.
2. Clear the **Review queue** — approve the good, set aside the rest.
3. Welcome and check **New pages**.

On a quiet wiki that's a couple of minutes now and then. The flags and the auto-moderator handle the obvious cases so your attention goes to the edits that actually need a human.
