# What is Wikigit?

Wikigit is a wiki — a website that a group of people build together, one page at a time. Think of a community handbook, a game guide, a team knowledge base, or an encyclopedia. Anyone you allow can add and improve pages, right in their browser.

What makes Wikigit different is how little there is to run and worry about.

## What you get

- **Free to host.** Your wiki runs on free services. There's no server to rent and no monthly bill to keep it online.
- **No database to manage.** Your pages are ordinary text files. Nothing can get "corrupted" in a way you can't see or fix.
- **Edit in the browser.** People click *Edit*, make a change, and save. No software to install, and — if you allow it — no account to create.
- **Nothing is ever lost.** Every change is saved with who made it and when, and any page can be rolled back to an earlier version in a click.
- **It's yours.** Your content lives in your own GitHub account. You can export it, move it, or take it elsewhere at any time. Nothing is locked inside Wikigit.
- **Looks the part.** A clean, familiar, Wikipedia-style reading experience out of the box, with light and dark modes and an optional skin that mirrors Wikipedia.

## Who it's for

You don't need to be a programmer. If you can fork a project on GitHub and click a couple of "deploy" buttons, you can run a Wikigit. The [setup guide](create-your-wiki.md) walks you through it.

It works well for:

- A community or fan wiki
- Documentation for a project or product
- A team or company knowledge base
- A personal notebook you want to publish

## How it works, briefly

You don't have to think about this to use Wikigit, but here's the short version:

Your wiki's pages live in a GitHub repository — a free, reliable place to store files. The website reads those pages and shows them to visitors. When someone makes an edit, a small helper service saves it back for them, so they never need their own GitHub account. Because the pages are just files, the website updates the moment a change is saved — there's no waiting and nothing to rebuild.

That's the whole system: your files, a website that shows them, and a helper that saves edits. The next page shows you how to set it up.
